Vector-based lighting and shading with subdivision surfaces

Abstract

Vector graphics has in recent years been drastically improved by diffusion-based interpolation of colours. The artist constrains the diffusion process via various types of curve primitives. We propose an alternative approach suited to lighting and shading. Our new shading curve primitive associates shading profiles out from both sides of a curve. These profiles are added to the image via subdivision surfaces in colour space. The resulting tonal variation out from a curve then follows its associated shading profile. We demonstrate results ranging from realistically 3D-looking renderings to chiaroscuro-looking drawings. We achieve interactive editing with our CPU implementation.
